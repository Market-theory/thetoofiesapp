Strava activity screen — anatomy

Reconstructed from public descriptions (not a screenshot). The point is the structure, not the exact pixels.
1Hero: 3 headline metrics

Map + the three numbers that matter — time, distance, pace — big and first. Same three on the Lock Screen. → Toofies: steps today + points banked.

2Recessive stat grid

Everything else (elevation, HR, cadence…) shrinks into small tiles. Hierarchy: 2–3 numbers dominate. → Toofies: quest progress + streak.

3Effort visualized over time

Pace overlaid on elevation — the run becomes a story, not a total. → Toofies: steps over the week, accumulating toward the next treat.

4Progressive disclosure

Splits and detail live below the fold for those who want them; the top stays clean.

5Completion is an event

Finishing is a moment (+ social kudos). → Toofies: the "quest complete / activity banked" celebration.

⚠ Anti-pattern

Their 2025 redesign was criticized for cramming map+stats+photos together. Keep Toofies' activity view focused.
