# Toofies — Product Vision

## The user's own words (2026-07-12)

> I love desserts… but I'm trying to be more healthy. I don't want to give up
> desserts entirely, but the goal is to be more responsible so I know when I
> had desserts most recently. In my mind, each day I don't eat dessert I get a
> certain amount of points, and after accumulating enough points, I can spend
> them by getting a dessert.

## Core mechanic — the points economy

- **Earn:** every completed dessert-free day banks **+10 points**, credited at
  midnight.
- **Spend:** logging a dessert costs points (default **30 = ~3 clean days**,
  adjustable 10–100). Save up across weeks for bigger occasions — points roll
  over, there is no weekly reset.
- **Forgiveness:** logging is never blocked and there is no debt. A dessert you
  can't afford spends the balance to zero and life goes on — honesty in
  logging always beats punishment (backed by the ED-risk literature in
  MARKET-RESEARCH.md).
- **Recency:** "when did I last have dessert" is a first-class surface — last
  dessert date, days ago, and clean-day streak.

## Framing rules (from research)

- Health/food app, positively framed: you **earn** treats; the app never
  scolds. No calories, no weight, no "diet" language, no hard streak resets.
- Playful tone (🦷 mascot, celebratory unlocks) — see
  `.planning/research/MARKET-RESEARCH.md` §2 and §6.

## Positioning

A health/food app for dessert lovers: responsible indulgence via an
earn-your-treat points economy. Differentiator vs CheatMate (logging only) and
No Treat Today (interval abstinence): the **transparent earn/spend economy
with a banked balance** — desserts feel *deserved*, not rationed.
