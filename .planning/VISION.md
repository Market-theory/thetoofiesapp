# Toofies — Vision

**This is the dream, captured — not a commitment.** Everything here is
aspirational and explicitly separate from what we decide to *build* and in what
order (that lives in `DECISION-REGISTER.md`, and v1 scope is still open). The
point of this doc is to hold the full ambition coherently so nothing is lost
and we can scope from it deliberately when you're ready.

## North star

Help dessert lovers enjoy the sweet things in life **without guilt** — by
making indulgence feel earned, social, and full of discovery. Never a diet app;
a celebration of dessert done responsibly and joyfully.

## The layers of the vision

1. **The personal loop (the wedge).** Earn points from dessert-free days and
   activity; spend them on desserts. Know exactly when you last indulged and
   when you've earned the next. Playful, forgiving, gamified (streaks, quests,
   milestones, "your sweet Toofies").
2. **The social layer.** Have desserts *with friends* — shared moments, maybe a
   feed, celebrating treats together. "Almost like a social media just about
   sharing your desserts" (founder, ideation transcript).
3. **The discovery layer.** Find *amazing* places to try new desserts —
   dedicated dessert shops **and restaurants with standout desserts** (often the
   best desserts live inside restaurants). Location-aware: what's great near me,
   or near my friends.
4. **The directory (the moat).** A robust, trustworthy directory of dessert
   destinations across the US — the definitive map of where to find the best
   sweet things.

## The beachhead strategy (why Orlando/Disney is smart)

A national directory has a brutal cold-start + freshness problem. The answer is
**not** to launch nationally — it's to **own one dessert-dense market first**:

- **Orlando / Walt Disney World** is an ideal beachhead: an enormous
  concentration of dessert destinations, a constant flow of tourists *actively
  hunting* desserts (high intent), and iconic, searchable spots (the Disney
  parks/resorts alone are a rich, self-contained dataset).
- Curate that one market deeply → prove the discovery loop with real users →
  then expand city by city (the Uber/DoorDash marketplace playbook).
- This makes the "impossible" national directory tractable: it becomes a
  sequence of winnable city launches, not one ocean to boil.

## What this vision implies (context for later scoping)

- Layers 2–4 require **accounts + a backend** (flips D4/D5), and layers 3–4
  add **location** (D23) and **place-data sourcing/licensing/freshness** (D24,
  D25) — real cost and ongoing maintenance.
- It also implies a **bigger ambition tier** (D3): a social discovery platform
  is venture-shaped, not a weekend indie app. That's a fork worth choosing
  deliberately, not drifting into.
- A natural, low-risk sequence exists if wanted: **v1** solo tracker (ship
  fast, prove the loop) → **v2** social/sharing → **v3** discovery, launched
  via the Orlando beachhead. But the sequence is yours to set.
