# Toofies — Eating-Disorder Specialist Review Brief

**For:** a registered dietitian and/or eating-disorder clinician.
**Ask:** review the core mechanic below for psychological safety and tell us
what to keep, change, or cut before we ship. This is a required gate — no
earn/spend food mechanic ships without your sign-off.

**~15-minute read. Please be blunt; we would rather hear "don't ship X" now.**

## What the app is

Toofies is a playful iOS app for dessert lovers who want to be **more careful
about their consumption without giving desserts up or dieting.** It is
deliberately **not** a calorie counter or weight-loss app — no calories, no
weight, no "diet" language. One of the founders has a personal history of
anorexia and designed the product explicitly *against* calorie tracking. The
primary job is peace of mind: "when did I last have a dessert, and am I doing
okay?"

## The mechanic we want you to review

There is an optional **points economy**:
- Each **dessert-free day** earns points (a base amount at midnight).
- With Apple Health connected, **steps** also earn points (activity → points),
  even on days you have dessert.
- Logging a dessert **spends** points. Points roll over; you can "save up."
- A **streak** counts consecutive "days on plan"; there are celebratory
  milestone moments. The design is deliberately **forgiving** — no debt, no hard
  reset, logging is never blocked.

## Our own concerns (please confirm, correct, or add)

An internal review flagged that despite the good no-calorie/no-weight intent,
the *structure* may recreate patterns the literature warns about:
1. **Restrict → reward:** you "earn" and "afford" dessert by abstaining. Is a
   points budget functionally a calorie budget in a friendlier unit?
2. **Exercise-as-penance:** steps earning dessert-points may install
   compensatory-exercise thinking ("I walked, so I can have it").
3. **Celebrating abstinence:** a streak that rewards dessert-*free* days may
   reinforce restriction as achievement.
4. **Optimal strategy = maximal restriction:** "hoard points, rarely spend"
   makes the most-restrictive user the most "successful."
5. **Reward = punishment moment:** enjoying a dessert makes the score go down.

## Safeguards we have already committed to

- No "cost / afford / can't afford / spend" language around food (wording TBD
  with your input).
- The score will not visibly drop as a *penalty* when a dessert is enjoyed.
- Streaks/milestones reframed around **balance and enjoyment**, not
  dessert-free days as a virtue score.
- A **"just track recency, no points"** mode; the economy is fully optional.
- Eating-disorder resource signposting in-app.

## The questions we need you to answer

1. **Is the core earn/spend-on-food mechanic safe enough to ship at all**, or is
   it fundamentally risky regardless of wording?
2. **Which specific elements must change or be removed** (the abstinence streak?
   steps-earn-food? the spend-depletes-score visual?)?
3. **Is the "no points, recency only" mode a sufficient safe default** — should
   the economy be opt-in, off by default, or cut for a first release?
4. **Copy/framing guidance:** what language keeps this on the healthy side of
   the line? What words must we never use?
5. **Screening/duty-of-care:** should the app detect or warn on patterns of
   over-restriction? What resources should we signpost, and how?
6. **Anything we haven't thought of.**

## What we'd love as output
A short note marking each mechanic element **keep / change / cut**, plus any
copy do's-and-don'ts and screening recommendations. We will treat this as
binding for the release.

## How to find a qualified reviewer (for the founder)

Look for a dietitian or clinician who **specializes in eating disorders** — a
generalist won't catch the subtle mechanic risks. Credentials that signal the
right expertise (verify current directories yourself; these are from general
knowledge as of early 2026):

- **CEDS / CEDS-S** — Certified Eating Disorder Specialist, credentialed by
  **iaedp** (International Association of Eating Disorders Professionals). Their
  member directory is the most targeted place to look.
- **CEDRD** — a registered dietitian with the eating-disorder specialty cert
  (dietitian-specific; ideal since our mechanic is food-behavior).
- Members of the **Academy for Eating Disorders (AED)**.
- The **Academy of Nutrition and Dietetics** "Find a Nutrition Expert" search,
  filtered for eating-disorder specialty (find an **RD/RDN**, then confirm ED focus).
- **NEDA** (National Eating Disorders Association) maintains referral resources.

**How to engage:** you're buying a **paid product/design safety review**, not
therapy — a 1–2 hour consult is standard. Budget a few hundred dollars; some
specialists offer "app/content review" explicitly. Send them this brief in
advance. If Toofies grows, consider an ongoing advisor relationship rather than
a one-time review.

**What good looks like:** they engage with the *specific* mechanic (not just
"tracking food is risky"), give you concrete keep/change/cut calls, and flag
things this brief didn't anticipate. If they only give generic caution, find
someone who'll go deep.

*Note: a specialist review reduces risk and is the right first step; it is not a
guarantee, and for a shipping consumer product ongoing clinical input is wise.*
