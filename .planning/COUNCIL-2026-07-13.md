# Toofies — Council Review (2026-07-13)

**Method:** five adversarial expert lenses, mandate = *find what's wrong, no
cheerleading*. Four ran as independent agents (VC, technical/data,
competitive/legal, product strategy); the behavioral-ethics lens was authored
in-house after that agent failed twice in this environment. The external-CLI
Council (Codex/Antigravity) was unavailable in the remote container, so this is
Claude-family analysis — not a true cross-vendor panel. Treat as strong input,
not gospel; the founder decides (per `CLAUDE.md`).

---

## Headline: a three-lens convergence

Three *independent* lenses — product strategy, behavioral ethics, and the VC —
arrived separately at the same place. That convergence is the strongest signal
in the review:

1. **The real, defensible, safe core is the recency / peace-of-mind tracker** —
   "when did I last have dessert? am I safe to have one?" — which is literally
   the first sentence of `PRODUCT.md`. Unserved by incumbents, on-device,
   founder-authentic.
2. **The points economy is the weakest and riskiest layer** — unproven (R5),
   behaviorally backwards (the inverted reward loop), and the part that carries
   the eating-disorder risk. It was *engineered before it was proven wanted.*
3. **The discovery / social / national-directory vision is the least
   defensible and highest-risk layer** — a commodity moat against world-class
   incumbents, with data-licensing and Disney-legal landmines.

When three unrelated critical lenses independently converge, weight it heavily.

## Per-lens verdicts

- **VC — PASS on venture; good indie.** TAM is capped by the (correct)
  no-weight-loss positioning; the discovery moat is a commodity; unit economics
  of a maintained directory are negative. The one defensible asset is the
  **ED-informed guilt-free framing/brand**, not the directory. Fund it as a
  bootstrapped indie/brand, not institutional venture.
- **Product strategy — NEEDS RESHAPE.** Ship the recency wedge; demote the
  economy to an experiment. The **inverted reward loop** (spend health-points
  on the indulgence) likely *reduces* retention — predicts drift at week 3–4
  when step novelty decays (the app's own Pokémon-GO/Mazeas evidence) and clean
  days offer no reason to open the app. Tracker (moderation mindset) and
  discovery (indulgence mindset) are two products for opposite emotional states.
- **Behavioral ethics (in-house) — RISKY BUT FIXABLE, one serious concern.**
  Despite the sincere no-calories/no-weight rules, the *structure* — you must
  "earn" and "afford" dessert by abstaining or exercising, and your score drops
  when you enjoy one — recreates the **restrict→reward** and **exercise-as-
  penance** patterns the ED literature warns about (Messer 2021: it's the
  framing/motive that harms, and a points budget is functionally a calorie
  budget in a different unit). Celebrating *abstinence* streaks is the sharpest
  risk. Given a founder with an ED history, safeguards below are non-negotiable
  and a clinical (RD/ED-specialist) review is warranted before shipping any
  earn/spend food mechanic.
- **Technical / data — the directory is the whole ballgame, and underestimated
  — but more buildable than the register feared.** Key unlock: **Foursquare
  open-sourced its global Places dataset (Apache 2.0, Nov 2024)** — a real,
  legal, *ownable* base layer that D25/R4's pessimism predates. Still an ongoing
  curation/freshness ops cost (the "great desserts" attribute exists in no
  dataset — it must be manufactured). Live location = safety liability → use
  **place check-ins, not live coordinates**. HealthKit data must stay on-device
  even in the accounts era (Guideline 5.1.3), bifurcating the data model.
- **Competitive / legal — discovery is oversaturated; directory is a feature,
  not a moat.** Google Maps, **Yelp's existing `/desserts` vertical**, **Beli
  (58M ratings, surpassed Yelp, <35 demo)**, and TikTok already own dessert
  discovery. Dedicated dessert apps exist and none broke out (weak-demand tell).
  **Disney issued a cease-and-desist wave to third-party businesses in Jan
  2026** — a *Disney-branded* beachhead is a lawsuit magnet; only the editorial,
  no-Disney-marks, disclaimed **Disney Food Blog model** survives.

## Highest-confidence convergences

1. **The recency wedge is the real product** (product, VC, behavioral).
2. **The points economy must be validated/A-B'd, not assumed** (product,
   behavioral, VC — and the register's own R5).
3. **Discovery/directory is not defensible as imagined** (VC, competitive,
   technical): buildable ≠ ownable ≠ a moat.
4. **Validate with real users before building more** (product, VC, R5).

## Genuine disagreements / nuance (where the signal is)

- **Data sourcing:** the technical lens (Foursquare OS Places makes the
  directory *buildable/ownable*) partly overturns the register's R4/D25
  pessimism — but the competitive lens counters that a buildable directory is
  still *not a moat*. Reconciliation: **buildable ≠ defensible.** Owning the
  data doesn't beat Google; only curation/community/taste might.
- **Ambition:** the VC says even the well-executed reshape is *not
  venture-scale*. If a venture outcome is the goal (D3), that's a real problem;
  if a profitable indie/brand is the goal, it's a feature. This is the D3 fork.

## New material facts surfaced (verify before relying)

- Foursquare OS Places dataset, Apache 2.0, released Nov 2024 (ownable base layer).
- Walt Disney World third-party cease-and-desist wave, Jan 2026.
- Beli: ~58M ratings (passed Yelp), <35 demographic, ~$12M raised.
- Yelp already runs a `/nearme/desserts` vertical.
- Google/Yelp/Foursquare *live-API* terms forbid building your own DB (Google:
  only `place_id` is storable long-term).

## What the council recommends (recommendations — NOT decisions)

1. **Ratify a lean, recency-first v1** (informs D2): the peace-of-mind tracker +
   widget, on-device, "Data Not Collected." Ship fast, prove demand (R5).
2. **Demote the full points economy (D7–D9) to an opt-in experiment.** A/B
   economy-vs-no-economy with real dessert-lovers; measure whether points make
   them open the app *more or less* (product lens predicts less).
3. **Behavioral safeguards are non-negotiable if the economy ships** (below),
   plus a clinical ED-specialist review.
4. **Discovery/social/directory = a deliberate later bet (D3), not v1.** If
   pursued: seed from Foursquare OS + curation; Orlando as a *tourist market*,
   never a Disney-branded app; **place check-ins, not live location.**
5. **Decide the D3 ambition fork honestly** — indie/brand vs venture — because
   the two imply completely different next 12 months.

## Behavioral safeguards (non-negotiable if any earn/spend food mechanic ships)

- **Never celebrate abstinence as the primary achievement.** Reframe the streak
  around *balance/enjoyment*, not dessert-free days as a virtue score.
- **No "cost / afford / can't afford / spend" language around food**, and the
  score must **not visibly drop as a penalty** at the moment of enjoying a
  dessert (that makes the reward moment a punishment).
- **Decouple food from compensatory exercise** — do not frame steps as "paying
  for" dessert; that installs exercise-as-penance.
- **Ship a "just track recency, no points" mode** so the economy is fully
  optional.
- **Signpost ED resources** and get a registered-dietitian / ED-specialist
  review before launch.
- Keep the existing no-calories/no-weight/forgiving rules — necessary, but the
  above shows they are **not sufficient**.
