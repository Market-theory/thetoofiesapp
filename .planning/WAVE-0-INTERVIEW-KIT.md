# Toofies — Wave 0 Interview Kit

Everything you need to run the demand/concept interviews in `VALIDATION-PLAN.md`
(Wave 0). Goal: find out if the **careful-consumption / recency** idea is real
for dessert-lovers, and whether the **points economy** reads as *fun* or as
*restriction*. Aim for 6–10 conversations, ~20 min each.

**The golden rule of these interviews: don't sell, don't lead.** You want the
truth, not politeness. Ask about their life first; show the concept late; stay
quiet and let them talk. If you find yourself explaining why it's good, stop.

---

## 1. Recruiting message (text / DM / post)

> Hey — I'm working on a little app for people who love dessert but want to be
> a bit more mindful about it (not a diet thing). Could I borrow 20 minutes to
> hear how you think about desserts? No prep, no right answers, and I'm not
> selling anything — I just want honest reactions. Free [coffee/gift card] for
> your time. 🍰

Recruit a deliberate mix: about half "I just love dessert and want to be a bit
more mindful," about half "I actively try to moderate sweets." A few locals
(Orlando) is fine but not required.

## 2. Screener (ask before booking, ~1 min)

1. How often do you have dessert? (Want a range across participants.)
2. Do you ever wish you were a little more mindful about it? (Light yes = ideal.)
3. Do you use any food/health tracking apps now? Which?
4. **Care screen (ask gently):** "Have you ever struggled with disordered
   eating or an eating disorder?" — Anyone who says yes (or current/recent) is
   **welcome for the recency conversation but is NOT shown the points economy**,
   and you offer resources (see ED-REVIEW-BRIEF.md). Their perspective on the
   *framing* is valuable; their exposure to the mechanic is not worth the risk.

## 3. Session setup (say this out loud)

> Thanks for doing this. There are no right answers and you won't hurt my
> feelings — blunt is better. I'll mostly listen and take notes. Okay if I jot
> things down? Nothing is recorded/shared. This is about how *you* think, not
> about the app.

## 4. Interview script

**A. Their world (no product yet — ~6 min)**
- "Tell me about the last time you had a dessert. Walk me through it — where,
  why, how you felt after."
- "Do you ever think about how recently you last had one? Tell me about that."
- "When you want to be 'good' about sweets, what does that look like for you?"
  *(Listen to their natural words — do THEY say guilt/earn/cheat, or balance/
  enjoy? This tells you a lot before you show anything.)*

**B. Concept A — recency, no points (~5 min)**
Show the simplest version: a screen that just says *"3 days since your last
dessert — you're doing great,"* one-tap to log a dessert.
- "What's your honest first reaction?"
- "Would this be useful, annoying, or neither? Why?"
- "When — if ever — would you open something like this?"

**C. Concept B — the points economy (~5 min)** *(skip for care-screened
participants)*
Show/describe: dessert-free days and steps earn points; you spend points to
have a dessert; a streak of "on-plan" days.
- "First reaction — fun, or does it feel like something else?"
- "How would you feel the day you 'spend' points on a dessert?"
- *(Say nothing leading. Then just listen for the language in §5.)*

**D. Preference & behavior (~3 min)**
- "If both existed, which would you actually keep on your phone — or neither?"
- "Would you tell a friend about it? Which part?"

**E. Wrap**
- "Anything about desserts or this idea I should've asked and didn't?"

## 5. What to listen for (write down their exact words)

**🚩 Red flags (especially on Concept B) — these outweigh enthusiasm:**
guilt, "cheat," "bad," "naughty," "earn it," "I'd have to work it off,"
"punish," feeling like a diet, anxiety, "I already feel bad enough." Any of
this on the economy is a **stop-and-reshape** signal, not a copy tweak.

**✅ Green flags:** "peace of mind," curiosity ("huh, I'd want to know"),
"fun/playful without the guilt," "this doesn't judge me," would-actually-use.
